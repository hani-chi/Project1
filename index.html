About Me

I'm Hanah, and I am a senior at Oakville High School. I currently attend St. Louis CAPS in the Technology strand, where we learn professional skills along with website and software development (HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and Jira). I'm skilled in UX/UI design and front-end development, which I am learning through personal research and through STLCAPS and Oasis Digital. I got into the tech industry through Girls Who Code, an organization that teaches girls how to code in hopes to close the gender gap in the industry. My next step is going to Webster University for Interactive Digital Design to help me become a front-end developer and UX/UI designer.

Dreams and Passions

Designer

Since I was a kid, art and design has been a big part of my life. One of my first dream careers was to be an interior designer. As I grew older, this branched off into architecture and more tech-related design careers, like graphic designer, website designer, and UX/UI designer. I didn't want to give up my love for art for more practical jobs and better pay, so I'd love a career that will let me continue my passion for design while working with tech.

Fashion

I have a passion for fashion; I've been putting outfits together by myself since I was young, and I've dabbled in many different aesthetics. This love for fashion made me consider becoming a stylist or a designer, but I don't think I'd want to monetize off of this passion in that way. Fashion is therapeutic for me, and shopping for a specific piece or style is exhilerating and just fun, to put it simply.

Cat Cafe

I fell in love with the idea of cat cafes after visiting one in my hometown for the first time, and I'd love to start one, too. I didn't just want to make a regular cat cafe, though; I've always had this desire to help communities and guide people into a better way of life. I plan to create a safe place or shelter for teens and women of all backgrounds that need any help. It would also serve as a donation center for clothes, food, and other basic needs.
